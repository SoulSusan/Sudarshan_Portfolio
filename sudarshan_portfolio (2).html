IEC 61400-11 Compliance & Acoustic Methodology

As part of the project, I participated in on-site IEC 61400-11 noise measurement campaigns on a multi-turbine wind farm. This involved capturing one-third octave band sound pressure levels across standardised wind speed bins, post-processing time-averaged spectra to identify tonal noise components from gearbox harmonics and blade trailing-edge broadband noise, and preparing a compliance-ready acoustic assessment report for environmental permitting.

The ML work then extended beyond compliance into predictive noise impact modelling: training classifiers to forecast the probability of resident complaints as a function of turbine operational state, time-of-day, atmospheric conditions, and seasonal context.

  • Third-octave SPL analysis across wind speed bins (6–10 m/s reference conditions)
  • Tonal noise detection: gearbox harmonics, blade passing frequency components
  • Correlation of SCADA yaw error with directional noise propagation
  • Machine learning model bridging acoustic output to resident feedback
  • Power BI dashboards for real-time noise pattern visualisation

Aerodynamic Design — BEM Theory

Blade design was built on Blade Element Momentum (BEM) theory, beginning with aerofoil selection from the NACA 64-series for high lift-to-drag performance in moderate-chord offshore conditions. Chord and twist distributions were optimised iteratively to maximise Cp across the operating wind speed range.

  • Aerofoil selection: NACA 64-618 at mid-span, flatback profiles at root for structural depth
  • Chord/twist optimisation via BEM iteration — Prandtl tip-loss correction applied
  • Cp = 0.488 at rated tip-speed ratio λ = 8.2
  • Annual Energy Production validated against FAST simulation outputs

Structural & Tower Design

Composite blade structural integrity was assessed using scaling laws and stiffness modelling for glass-fibre / carbon-fibre layup. Tower design resolved 1P/3P modal resonance conflicts — a critical compliance requirement for offshore foundations — through iterative geometry optimisation.

  • Composite blade stiffness: flapwise / edgewise / torsional modes verified
  • Allowable stress limits maintained across extreme turbulence load cases
  • Tower natural frequency placed in "soft-stiff" region between 1P and 3P
  • Monopile foundation sizing for 40 m water depth conditions

Drivetrain Engineering

The drivetrain concept specified a two-stage planetary gearbox coupled to a permanent-magnet synchronous generator (PMSG), with full-converter power electronics. Gearbox ratio, generator pole-pair count, and converter rating were designed as an integrated system to minimise nacelle mass while achieving rated power output with acceptable efficiency losses.

  • Two-stage planetary gearbox: ratio 1:75, rated torque 1.28 MNm at rotor shaft
  • PMSG: 4 MW/m³ power density, low cogging torque for variable-speed operation
  • Full-converter topology: decoupled grid-side / machine-side control
  • Nacelle mass budget: 350 tonnes — within offshore installation crane limits

Physics-Informed Feature Engineering

Each bearing fault type has a characteristic frequency signature determined by component geometry. The inner race defect frequency (BPFI), outer race frequency (BPFO), and ball spin frequency (BSF) are analytically calculable — making them ideal physics-informed feature targets.

  • FFT amplitude at BPFI/BPFO/BSF harmonics — direct physical fault signature
  • Hilbert envelope analysis for amplitude modulation in early-stage faults
  • Kurtosis, crest factor, RMS — sensitive to impulsive fault events
  • Spectral entropy: structural disorder indicator for progressive degradation
  • Rolling window features capturing temporal health trajectory → RUL signal

Materials Characterisation

Working in a cleanroom environment (Kavli-Nano Lab, TU Delft), advanced analytical techniques were applied to study aluminium electrode corrosion, passivation layer formation, and electrochemical degradation under controlled discharge conditions — phenomena mechanistically analogous to wind turbine hub casting corrosion and tower steel fatigue crack initiation.

  • Raman spectroscopy: phase identification and corrosion product characterisation
  • SEM-EDS: surface morphology and elemental composition of degraded interfaces
  • ICP-OES: quantitative dissolution tracking of structural alloy elements
  • Controlled discharge testing: voltage/current/temperature monitoring over extended cycles
  • Failure mode documentation: 40% reduction in root-cause analysis time

Cradle-to-Grave LCA

A full lifecycle assessment was conducted using ISO 14044 methodology, covering raw material extraction, manufacturing, operational phase, and end-of-life recyclability. The LCA framework, including inventory modelling, impact assessment, sensitivity analysis, and hotspot identification, is directly transferable to wind turbine system LCAs required for environmental permitting and sustainability reporting.

  • System boundary: cradle-to-grave including recycling and landfill pathways
  • Impact categories: GWP, acidification, freshwater eutrophication, resource depletion
  • Sensitivity analysis: material substitution scenarios for nacelle alloys
  • Hotspot identification: manufacturing stage dominates — 73% of total GWP

The Core Challenge

Acoustic noise models tell you how loud a turbine is. They say nothing about whether anyone will care. Annoyance depends on time of day, wind direction, ambient background noise, whether windows are open, whether people are asleep which is a deeply contextual, human problem.

The key insight was to treat this as a lifestyle-driven time-series forecasting problem layered on top of a physics model. By correlating turbine operational state with patterns in when people typically sleep, work from home, or spend time outdoors, we could predict annoyance probability as a joint function of energy output and social context.

Feature Engineering Strategy

The critical work was constructing features that bridge the physical and the human. Raw RPM and decibel values alone have weak predictive power. What matters is contextualised signal.

  • Time-of-day encoding (sinusoidal, to capture cyclical sleep patterns)
  • Wind direction × residential zone bearing (is turbine upwind of residents?)
  • RPM rate-of-change: sudden spin-up events correlate with sharp annoyance spikes
  • Ambient background noise proxy from weather (rain, traffic)
  • Weekend/weeknight flag: sensitivity is demonstrably higher on Sunday nights
  • Rolling complaint lag features: prior complaints predict near-term complaints

From Vibration to Intelligence

Raw vibration signals from accelerometers are noise. The engineering work is in the transformation: converting time-domain waveforms into frequency-domain features that expose the characteristic fault frequencies of bearing components.

Each bearing fault type has a physical signature determined by geometry with inner race defect frequency (BPFI), outer race frequency (BPFO), ball spin frequency (BSF). Knowing this, we designed features that encode physics, not just statistics.

  • Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) amplitude at BPFI/BPFO/BSF harmonics
  • Envelope analysis via Hilbert transform for amplitude modulation detection
  • Statistical features: kurtosis, crest factor, RMS which are sensitive to impulsive events
  • Spectral entropy: measures structural disorder in frequency distribution
  • Rolling window features capturing progression over time → RUL signal

RUL Forecasting

Beyond fault classification, the system estimates Remaining Useful Life translating a probabilistic health score into an actionable maintenance window. This allows operators to plan maintenance during scheduled production pauses rather than reacting to sudden failures.

A regression model trained on historical run-to-failure cycles learns to map the trajectory of feature degradation to expected time-to-failure, outputting a confidence-bounded RUL estimate updated with each new data window.

  • Classification: Random Forest, 4-class, 90% accuracy / 0.9 recall
  • RUL regression: Gradient Boosted regression on health index trajectory
  • Cloud connectivity: real-time streaming to monitoring dashboard
  • Alert thresholds: configurable by maintenance team per asset type

System Architecture

The simulation runs in two layers. The physical layer (MATLAB/Simulink) models the solar PV array orientation, tilt, string configuration, panel I-V curves and the BESS charge/discharge cycle with SoC constraints, degradation limits, and inverter efficiency curves.

The economic layer (Python) ingests historical day-ahead market prices from ENTSO-E, synthetic consumer profiles generated from aggregate residential load shape data, and the physical simulation outputs to compute a minute-level power balance and translate it into a rolling hourly electricity cost.

  • Solar PV: orientation optimisation (azimuth + tilt), series/parallel string config to stay within inverter current limits
  • BESS: SoC-bounded dispatch with peak-shaving and arbitrage logic
  • Pricing: ENTSO-E day-ahead prices, multi-year historical validation
  • Consumption: synthetic profiles from standardised residential load shapes, parameterised by household size and season

The Dashboard

The final output is a Python Dash application with Plotly-driven charts that give a household (or building manager) complete visibility over their energy economics updated in near real-time as solar generation and price data flows in.

Key interface panels include: today's forecast vs. yesterday's actuals, battery SoC trajectory, grid import/export balance, and a cumulative cost comparison between BESS-optimised and unoptimised operation. Configurable inputs allow users to model different tariff structures, battery sizes, and consumption profiles.

  • Hourly cost forecasting with confidence intervals
  • Battery dispatch optimisation overlay
  • Price spike alert system (configurable threshold)
  • Seasonal performance comparison: summer vs. winter solar yield
  • Export-to-grid revenue tracking
